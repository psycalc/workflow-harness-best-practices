# Fourth Function

**Source:** https://sites.google.com/view/psychosophy/main-theory-official/functions/fourth-function  
**Author:** Alexander Yuryevich Afanasyev  
**Compiled by:** Psychosophy Library

---

## Abbreviated Characteristics

**"Trifle", Result-oriented, Scant, Monologue-oriented, Rigid, Indifferent, Fearless, Unreliable, Dependent.**

## Key Properties

### 1. "Trifle" Nature

It is a **"trifle"** — a function to which we ourselves attach little importance.

> "It doesn't mean that the Fourth is weak and unproductive. The main thing is the subjective hierarchy of internal values, forcing one thing in itself to be placed on the last fourth step."

**4th Logic is not necessarily stupid, 4th Emotion is not necessarily insensitive, etc.**

### 2. Similarity to Second

Externally, the Fourth function is difficult to distinguish from the Second. They are related by **freedom, naturalness** and **fearlessness of self-expression**.

However, there is a deep difference: a person with 2nd Logic will not be offended by criticism because they won't believe they are wrong, and someone with 4th Logic — because they're deeply **indifferent** to the assessments in this part.

### 3. Means, Not End

Activities of the Fourth function don't have any **independent value**, it is **not an end, but a means of existence**.

Therefore, if someone with the 4th Logic is engaged in sphere of intellectual labor, they're trying to use this function as a tool with the help of which the requests of other self-valuable higher functions are realized.

### 4. Unreliable

Information that is extracted and obtained by the Fourth can be seen as **unreliable**.

### 5. Turns Off in Crisis

The Fourth function **completely turns off in crisis situations**. The human psyche turns it off as a possible hindrance in choosing the right decision.

### 6. Mirrors Others

The Fourth function **mirrors other people's requests**. Interaction with the Fourth is always identical to the order made by the superior function of the partner.

### 7. Dependent and Pliable

The fourth function is **very dependent** and **easily entrusts itself to other functions** higher in the hierarchy.

> "The fourth function is a **slave without guile**, a **chameleon** and a **dependent** with a large, but more often dormant potential."
