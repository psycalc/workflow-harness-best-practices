# Second Function

**Author:** Alexander Yuryevich Afanasyev  
**Compiled by:** Psychosophy Library

## Abbreviated Characteristics

**"River", Process-oriented, Normative, Dialogue-oriented, Flexible, Pitiful, Fearless, Confident, Compromising.**

## Key Properties

### 1. Normative Nature

The Second function is **NORMATIVE**. Like any norm, it is difficult to describe. It is unlikely that a person will find more than two words when talking about something if it's normal.

### 2. "River" Metaphor

If the First Function is best compared to a **"hammer"**, then the Second to a **"river"**.

### 3. Gift to Others

The Second function is our **gift to others** and the **best side of a person**. It has qualities unknown to the First: **breadth, wealth, naturalness** and **flexibility**. The source of these virtues lies in the fact that in the Second, strength is connected with orientation to process — it's a force charged for constant dialogue, constant interaction.

### 4. Rich in Hues

The second function is **extremely rich in hues** and has an enormous range. In order not to go far, one researcher calculated that Tolstoy described 85 shades of expression in the eyes and 97 shades of a smile.

### 5. Process-Oriented

Another characteristic is that the completeness of its implementation is possible only if there is a **partner, an audience**.

### 6. Pity and Compassion

The combination of **strength** and **orientation to process** endows the Second Function with such an unusually valuable quality as **pity**. The specificity of the Second's pity lies precisely in the fact that it pities from the position of **strength, confidence and experience**.

### 7. Fearless

A person experiences a kind of pleasure, putting it to the test often in dubious and even obviously uncomfortable situations. The Second one is not only strong, but also flexible, so attacks to it do not hurt, but only give direction to work on its further improvement.
