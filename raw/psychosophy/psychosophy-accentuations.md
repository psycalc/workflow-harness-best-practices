## What are Accentuations?

Working on the "Imperative Socionics" project, researchers discovered that carriers of the same psychosophic functions may exhibit different character traits depending on which properties of the functions are strengthened.

**An accentuation means that your function can manifest itself differently based on the strength of one of the three main characteristics.**

## The Three Dichotomies

Each function has 3 characteristics (dichotomies):

| Dichotomy | Positions | Description |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Result vs Process** | Result: 1, 4 / Process: 2, 3 | Focus on stable states vs ongoing activities |
| **Strong vs Weak** | Strong: 1, 2 / Weak: 3, 4 | Independent vs influenced by others |
| **Introverted vs Extraverted** | Introverted: 1, 3 / Extraverted: 2, 4 | Directed to own state vs others' state |

## Example: 4V Accentuations

For types with the Fourth Volition (4V), three main behavioral models exist:

### 1. Weak Accentuation (4V-3)
The owner of 4V drifts with the flow, does not make independent decisions and is subject to any external influences. He is unsure of himself, weak-willed and "lethargic." In relationships, he is very submissive.

### 2. Result Accentuation (4V-1)
He is strongly subject to his own desires, does what comes to mind, and is not inclined to self-restraint. He can be mistaken for 1V, but differs in his dislike for responsibility.

### 3. Introverted Accentuation (4V-2)
He supports those around him, actively interested in their goals and desires, and strives for compromise. He differs from 2V in that he is inclined to compromise his own interests and sacrifice himself.

## How Accentuations Work

> "We came to the conclusion that such a term as 'accentuation' can be introduced into psychosophy, which will mean the most vivid manifestation of one of the three properties that a function has."

> "To one degree or another, all these qualities are expressed in each function, and the predominance of one of them forms the accentuation of the function."

A function with accentuated "Weak" property looks more like the function that shares that property (3F in case of 4F).

## Notation

The subtypes by BestSocionics use property names:
- "Weak" / "Strong"
- "Result"
- "Introverted" / "Extraverted"

Online forums also use numerical notation: "4F-3" (meaning 4F with accentuated Weak property, like 3F)
