## 1. Smart Mobilization: Technology-Enabled Military Personnel Management

### 1.1 Definition

"Smart mobilization" refers to the use of AI, big data analytics, and automation to optimize recruitment, selection, and personnel distribution in armed forces during both peacetime and wartime.

### 1.2 Key Examples (2024-2026)

#### Recruit 360 (US Army, 2024)
- Analyzes 30 million candidate files using 1,700 variables
- Identifies propensity for service before recruiter contact
- Currently tested in 5 cities

#### People Supply Chain Management (PSCM) (US Army, 2025)
- Commercial supply chain principles applied to soldier training pipeline
- AI-powered demand forecasting across 200+ MOS
- Real-time inventory optimization

#### PLA Air Force AI Biometric Screening (China, 2025)
- AI interprets biological signals of pilot candidates
- Predicts long-term health risks
- Human-machine adaptability assessment
- "Dual-engine" model: data intelligence + human expertise

### 1.3 Benefits

- Predictive analytics replacing reactive recruitment
- Reduced time-to-hire from months to weeks
- Better candidate-to-position matching
- Workforce agility for emerging capabilities

## 2. Personality Typology in Military Context

### 2.1 MBTI (Myers-Briggs Type Indicator)

#### Origins (WWII)
- **Created during World War II** by Katharine Cook Briggs and Isabel Briggs Myers (1943)
- **Purpose**: Help women entering industrial workforce find "most comfortable and effective" wartime jobs
- **Historical note**: First used by **Office of Strategic Services (OSS)** — CIA predecessor — for assigning intelligence operatives in liberated Europe (1944-1945)
- First published as "Briggs Myers Type Indicator Handbook" in 1944
- Standard "MBTI" designation established in 1956

#### Application History
- Used for decades in US military for officer development
- Assists in career branch selection
- Leadership style assessment

#### WWII Effectiveness Data

- **80% success rate** in combat positions (Peabody et al., 1946)
- Personnel selected through evaluations were **50% more effective** in their roles (Army Air Forces, 1949)
- Post-war Army commissioned pivotal APA study confirming validity

#### Research Findings (1986-2012)

| Study Population | Dominant Types | Key Insight |
|----------------|---------------|------------|
| Military executives | 80% TJ (Thinking-Judgers) | Situational demands selecting for specific types |
| Combat officers | ISTJ, ESTJ, ENTJ, INTJ | Effective military leaders |
| Peacetime army | SJ types | Prefer stability, procedures |
| Wartime army | SP, NT types | Prefer action, innovation |

#### Peacetime vs Wartime Shift
- **Peacetime**: SJ (Sensing-Judgers) dominate — detail-oriented, dependable, punctual
- **Wartime**: NP, NT types fill quickly — adventurous, decisive, innovative, imaginative

### 2.2 Big Five (Five-Factor Model)

#### Dutch Special Forces (2022)
- Commandos less neurotic (d = -0.58)
- More conscientious (d = 0.45)
- Markedly less open to experiences (d = -1.13)
- Selection discriminant validity limited; other factors needed

#### German KSK Special Forces (2026)
- Conscientiousness correlates with success (r = -0.74)
- Cognitive performance correlates with success (r = -0.72)
- Self-regulation resources (mental visualization, arousal control) important
- Grit and resilience as protective factors

#### Ukrainian Military (2024)
- Emotional stability: strongest predictor of psychological wellbeing
- Conscientiousness and openness linked to higher engagement
- Self-efficacy and resilience mitigate PTSD symptoms

## 4. Challenges and Limitations

- **Ethics**: Personal data privacy in AI recruitment
- **Validity**: MBTI criticized for scientific rigor
- **Bias**: AI tools may reproduce existing biases
- **Faking**: Candidates can distort personality test results
