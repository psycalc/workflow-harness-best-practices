# Existing Scientific Validation Research

## MBTI Research (Well-Validated)

### Reliability Meta-Analysis
| Study | Sample | Finding |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **Capraro (2002)** | Meta-analysis | Internal consistency 0.80-0.87, test-retest reliable |
| **Randall et al. (2017)** | 7 studies | Construct validity reasonable, test-retest .75+ for E-I, S-N, J-P |
| **Erford et al. (2025)** | 193 studies, n=57,170 | Internal consistency 0.845-0.921 |

### Key MBTI Findings
- KR-20s: E-I=0.91, S-N=0.92, T-F=0.91, J-P=0.92 (MBTI Manual)
- CFA: GFI=0.949, NFI=0.967 (excellent fit)
- Test-retest 6-15 weeks: 0.81 correlation

## Socionics Research (Emerging)

### Peer-Reviewed Publications

#### 1. Pietrak (2017/2018) — Foundation Review
**"The Foundations of Socionics – A Review"**
- Published: *Cognitive Systems Research* (Elsevier, Q1 journal)
- DOI: 10.1016/j.cogsys.2017.07.001
- **Download:** SSRN: https://ssrn.com/abstract=2970140

**Abstract:** Discusses Aušra Augustinavičiūtė's model, information metabolism, Reinin's experiments, Horwood & Maw study.

**Key Studies Reviewed:**
- Reinin (2005): Psychological and somatic effects of type combinations
- Horwood & Maw (2012): Theater teams, socionics improves team cohesiveness
- Eglit (2013): Personality disorders explained via information metabolism

#### 2. Pietrak (2017) — Comprehensive Review
**"Review of the Socionic Model of Information Metabolism at Individual, Interpersonal and Societal Levels"**
- SSRN: https://ssrn.com/abstract=3001323
- 48 pages, 118 references
- Critically reviews philosophical underpinnings, intertype relationships

#### 3. Kovalenko & Zvonareva (2020)
**"Correlation between socionic type and coefficients of empathy (EQ) and systematization (SQ)"**
- Published: *Psychologist* journal (Russia)
- DOI: 10.25136/2409-8701.2020.3.32751
- **Finding:** Correlations between "logic/ethics" dichotomy and EQ/SQ confirmed

#### 4. Kovalenko & Zvonareva (2021)
**"Interrelation between the socionic type and Big Five personality traits"**
- Published: *Psychologist* journal (Russia)
- DOI: 10.25136/2409-8701.2021.3.35353
- **Finding:** Socionic characteristics correlate with established personality measures

#### 5. Kovalenko (2025) — Couples Study
**"A typological approach to analyzing psychological compatibility in romantic relationships"**
- DOI: 10.12731/2658-4034-2025-16-4-807
- Sample: 95 married/long-term couples (2015-2023)
- **Finding:** Supports Bukalov & Filatova's structural compatibility patterns
- Proposes: Shift from categorical to dimensional (trait-based) compatibility model

## Applied Studies

### Horwood & Maw (2012) — Theater Teams
**"Theatre teams assembled using personality profiles can improve predicted teamworking scores"**
- Published: *Annals of the Royal College of Surgeons*
- DOI: 10.1308/147363512X13189526439791
- Sample: 6 theater teams in UK hospital
- **Finding:** Socionics-based team composition significantly improves teamworking scores

### Bukalov, Karpenko, Chikirisova (1998)
**"Statistics of married couples"**
- Sample: 119 married couples
- **Finding:** 45% duality, 64% intra-quadra — confirms intertype predictions

### Filatova (2000)
**"Socionics type and intertype relations statistics"**
- Sample: 105 families, 299 people
- **Finding:** Children tend to be dual to mothers (25.7%), identical to fathers (28.7%)

## Critical Perspectives

### Why Socionics Lags Behind MBTI in Acceptance

1. **Lack of Western empirical studies** — Most research in Russian
2. **Pseudoscience classification** — Russian Academy of Sciences placed it with astrology
3. **No standardized typing method** — Different schools use different methods
4. **Speculative categories** — Information metabolism not operationally defined
5. **Publishing fragmentation** — Unsystematic publications

### What MBTI Did Right

1. Published extensively in English
2. Created standard test instruments (MBTI Form M)
3. Maintained corporate backing (CPP)
4. Conducted large-scale normative studies
5. Systematic research program over decades

## What Still Needs to Be Done

### Gaps in Literature

| Gap | Current State | What's Needed |

| **Predictive validity** | Some couples studies | Pre-registered longitudinal |
| **Cross-cultural** | Mostly Russian | Global replication |
| **Mechanism** | Theoretical | fMRI, process tracing |

2. **Horwood & Maw team study** in other industries
3. **Big Five correlation** — Expand Kovalenko's work
