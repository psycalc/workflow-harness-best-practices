# What is Socionics

Socionics is a typology founded by Lithuanian sociologist Aušra Augustinavičiūtė, based on Carl Jung's typology and extended with Model A theory. Unlike MBTI, Socionics describes 16 types using 8 cognitive functions (IM elements) arranged in a specific structure.

## Core Concepts

**Information metabolism** is the process of assimilating, processing, and transferring information by the human psyche. Socionics is also known as the theory of information metabolism (TIM - Type of Information Metabolism).

**Information** in socionics refers to data characterizing phenomena and processes in our world. The subject of research is how the psyche perceives, processes, and assimilates this information.

**Model A** is the interconnected structure of eight functions. Each function processes different aspects of information with different properties.

## The 16 Socionics Types

The types are organized into four quadras, each with shared values:

| Quadra | Values | Types |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Alpha** | Joy, playfulness, ideas, principles | ILE, LII, ESE, SLI |
| **Beta** | Power, justice, emotions, ideals | SLE, LIE, EIE, LSI |
| **Gamma** | Business, results, objects, facts | SIE, ILI, ESI, LSE |
| **Delta** | Knowledge, services, harmony, time | IEE, EII, SEE, LII |
