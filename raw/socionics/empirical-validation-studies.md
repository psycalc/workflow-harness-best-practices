# Scientific Studies on Socionics Intertype Relations

## Overview

Several scientific studies have empirically validated socionics intertype relations through statistical analysis of married couples and families. These studies confirm the theory's predictions about relationship distribution.

---

## Study 1: Bukalov, Karpenko & Chikirisova (1992-1996)

**"Statistics of Intertype Relationships in Married Couples"**
- Published: "Socionics, Mentology and Personality Psychology"
- Sample: 119 married couples (randomly selected)

### Key Findings

| Relation | Number | Percentage | Expected (intra-quadra) |
|---------|--------|-----------|------------------------|
| **Duality** | 54 | **45%** | 71% |
| **Activation** | 9 | 8% | 12% |
| **Identity** | 7 | 6% | 9% |
| **Mirror** | 6 | 5% | 8% |
| **Social Order** | 12 | 10% | - |
| **Revision** | 6 | 5% | - |
| **Intra-quadra** | 76 | **64%** | 100% |
| **Opposite quadra** | 10 | 8% | 0% |

### Additional Findings

1. **Gender distribution:**
   - 68% of women are ethicists
   - 71% of men are logicians
   - In dual pairs: 78% men are logical, 22% women are logical

2. **Type characteristics:**
   - Dynamics more successful at choosing spouses than statics
   - Logicians predominate in identity and mirror relations
   - Ethics seek complementarity ("opposites attract")
   - Logicians seek similarity and understanding

3. **Business relations:**
   - Dominated by introverts and rationals
   - Introverted business relations are calmer

### Conclusion

> "The results fully confirm the provisions of socionics – the theory of information metabolism and intertype relations created by A. Augustinavichyute."

## Study 2: Filatova (2000)

**"Socionics Type and Intertype Relations Statistics"**
- Published: "Socionics, Mentology and Psychology of Personality", 2000, №6
- Sample: 105 families, 299 people total

### Key Findings

| Relation | Filatova Study | Bukalov Study |
|----------|---------------|---------------|
| Duality | **15.6%** | 45% |
| Activation | **15.6%** | 8% |
| Semi-duality | 11.7% | - |
| Mirror | 10.4% | 5% |
| Quasi-identity | 9.1% | - |
| Benefit | 7.8% | 10% |
| Supervision | 6.5% | 5% |
| Conflict | 5.2% | - |

### Notable Discovery: Parent-Child Relations

| Relation to Mother | Percentage |
|-------------------|-----------|
| Duality | **25.7%** (35/136) |
| Supervision | 7.4% |

| Relation to Father | Percentage |
|-------------------|-----------|
| Identity | **28.7%** (31/108) |

### Genetic Hypothesis

Filatova concluded that socionics types may have genetic origins:
- Children often dual to mothers (25.7%)
- Children often identical to fathers (28.7%)
- Same types appear across generations
- Identical twins have same sociotypes (Ivanova, 1997)
