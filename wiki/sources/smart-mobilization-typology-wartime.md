# Smart Mobilization and Typology Application in Wartime

Research compilation on the intersection of AI-enabled military personnel management and personality typology applications in wartime contexts.

## Key Concepts

### Smart Mobilization

Technology-enabled approaches to military recruitment, selection, and personnel management:

- **AI-driven recruitment**: Tools like Recruit 360 (US Army, 2024) analyzing millions of candidate profiles
- **Predictive logistics**: Systems using AI for force planning and resource optimization
- **Biometric screening**: AI-powered physiological assessment (PLA Air Force, 2025)
- **Digital phenotyping**: Real-time mental health monitoring via personal devices

### Personality Typology in Military Context

Multiple frameworks applied to military personnel selection:

- **MBTI**: Created during WWII (1943) by Katharine Cook Briggs and Isabel Briggs Myers; first used by OSS for intelligence operative placement; 80% success rate in combat positions
- **Big Five**: Research-driven selection for special forces
- **Value typologies**: Combat stress resilience assessments

## Main Findings

1. **Technology integration**: "Dual-engine" model combines AI data processing with human expert evaluation
2. **Peacetime/wartime shift**: Different personality types dominate in different operational contexts
3. **Predictive validity**: Combination of traits outperforms single measures
4. **Special forces profiles**: Lower neuroticism, higher conscientiousness, lower openness to experience
