# Psychosophy: Operational Level — Comprehensive Analysis

> **Level:** Operational  
> **Latent Process:** Analysis, synthesis, and action organization  
> **Frame Type:** Action frame  
> **Core Question:** "How do we get things done together?"

Psychosophy (also known as Attitudinal Psyche or Psyche-Yoga) is a typology created by Alexander Afanasyev, first published in his 1993 book "Syntax of Love" (Синтаксис любви). The system describes how a person works with tasks, goals, methods of action, and role distribution.

**Key source:** The Russian original is at psychosophy.ru; an English adaptation called "Attitudinal Psyche" was created by Rob Collopy.

From bestsocionics.com: *"The structure of psychosophy was first described by the author of typology, Russian writer Alexander Afanasiev, in his book 'Syntax of Love'. The fundamental basis in psychosophy is a model, represented by four functions, set by unique combinations of three pairs of properties."*

**Important context from Reddit r/Psychosophy:**
> "Psychosophy comes from Russian mystic/philosophical tradition. Reality is conceived as a living whole where human consciousness participates in divine or cosmic order. Modern Western psychology emerged from empiricism, positivism, and Cartesian dualism. The focus is on observable behavior, cognitive processes, and measurable traits."

## Quadra System (6 Quadras)

Psychosophy has **6 quadras**, each containing 4 fixed types. In the source material, quadras are defined by **exact type clusters**, not by a simple "first aspect = quadra" rule.

| Quadra | Types |
|-------|-------|
| **1** | ЭЛВФ, ЭВЛФ, ФВЛЭ, ФЛВЭ |
| **2** | ЭФВЛ, ЭВФЛ, ЛВФЭ, ЛФВЭ |
| **3** | ЭЛФВ, ЭФЛВ, ВФЛЭ, ВЛФЭ |
| **4** | ЛЭФВ, ЛФЭВ, ВФЭЛ, ВЭФЛ |
| **5** | ВЛЭФ, ВЭЛФ, ФЛЭВ, ФЭЛВ |
| **6** | ФВЭЛ, ФЭВЛ, ЛЭВФ, ЛВЭФ |

**Source:** Psychosophy Library / Psychosophy theory pages

### Determining Your Quadra

Your type format: `[1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th]`

- Determine quadra by the **full 4-letter type**, not by the first aspect alone.
- Example: **ЭЛВФ** is **Quadra 1**.
- Example: **ФВЛЭ** is **Quadra 1**.
- Example: **ВПНБ** does not apply here; that is Temporistics.

## Theoretical Foundation

### The Four Aspects

| Aspect | Symbol | Domain | Real-World Manifestation |
|--------|--------|--------|-------------------------|
| **Logic** | L | Truth, systems, structure, reasoning | Planning, problem-solving, truth-seeking |
| **Emotion** | E | Feelings, atmosphere, relationships | Emotional expression, empathy, social bonding |
| **Will (Volition)** | V | Goals, decisions, control, initiative | Leadership, commitment, determination |
| **Physics** | F | Material world, comfort, resources | Physical needs, money, household, routine |

### The Four Functions (Positions)

From bestsocionics.com:

| Function | Properties | Primary Quality |
|----------|------------|-----------------|
| **1st** | Subjective, Unconscious, Introverted | Result-oriented, principled |
| **2nd** | Subjective, Conscious, Extraverted | Process-oriented, flexible |
| **3rd** | Objective, Conscious, Introverted | Process-oriented, principled |
| **4th** | Objective, Unconscious, Extraverted | Result-oriented, flexible |

**The Key Insight (from psychosophy library):**
> "A person can and wants to influence other people through the aspects of high functions, but they themselves are not subject to influence. In turn, regarding the aspects of the objective functions, a person cannot influence others but is subject to influence by those around them."

## Part I: Logic Function (L)

**Domain:** Truth, correctness, structure, systems, knowledge

### 1L — Primary Modeling

**Properties:** Resultative, Principled, Subjective

**Latent process:** "I quickly broke down → quickly assembled → gave ready working contour"

**From bestsocionics.com:**
> "The first function is high, productive and principled. For this function one's own aspectual state is a priority... Forms its state independently."

**Real-world example (mortgage decision):**
- **Analysis:** "Look, here are 3 banks, 2 types of rates, hidden commissions, here's the income risk"
- **Synthesis:** "So the optimal scheme is this. We take option B"
- Person breaks down AND assembles final model themselves

**Forum discussions (Typology Central):**
> "1L types are confident in their logical frameworks. They don't need external verification because their internal model feels 'correct.'"

**In relationships:**
- Natural leader on logical planning and problem-solving
- May not understand why others can't see obvious structure
- Can overwhelm partner with premature conclusions
- Good with 4L partners who accept their frameworks

**Compatibility patterns:**
- 1L + 4L: One builds scheme, other accepts it
- 1L + 2L: Both contribute to logical understanding
- 1L + 3L: 1L may push 3L to conclude before ready

### 2L — Cooperative Modeling

**Properties:** Processive, Flexible, Subjective

**Latent process:** "Parsed together → assembled together → adapted for two"

**Real-world example:**
- **Analysis:** "Let's look together. You worry more about the monthly payment, I worry about overpayment. OK, let's divide criteria"
- **Synthesis:** "Then for us the best scheme is this, because it balances both priorities"
- Synthesis and analysis go cooperatively

**From The Typendium:**
> "Some 'logics' tend to be more guided by their own conclusions, while others—by the opinion of authoritative sources."

**In relationships:**
- Natural partner for joint planning and problem-solving
- Values collaborative logical process
- Can adapt logical approach to partner's needs
- Good with any type that values joint decision-making

### 3L — Anxious Modeling

**Properties:** Processive, Principled, Objective

**Latent process:** "Analyzes too long → doubts → poorly glues into stable whole"

**Real-world example:**
- **Analysis:** "Wait, what if we didn't account for insurance? What if I misunderstood the terms? What if the conclusion is completely wrong?"
- **Synthesis:** Person cannot assemble final scheme due to persistent uncertainty

**From bestsocionics.com:**
> "The third function is low, processional and principal. This function determines the carrier's constant attention to its aspects."

**In relationships:**
- May need support in reaching logical conclusions
- May second-guess decisions repeatedly
- Can be exhausting for partners who prefer quick decisions
- Needs patient partner who doesn't rush conclusion

**Warning signs:** Excessive consultation of sources, inability to commit to plans, "analysis paralysis"

### 4L — Frame-Taking

**Properties:** Resultative, Unprincipled, Objective

**Latent process:** "Minimally analyzes → takes already assembled → acts within it"

**Real-world example:**
- **Analysis:** "Explain briefly what's the difference"
- **Synthesis:** "OK, understood. If you checked it, we take this option"
- Doesn't build complex model independently

**From bestsocionics.com (4th function descriptions):**
> "Fourth Logic individuals are those who are lazy to think. They tend to adopt someone else's opinion, and in everyday life, they filter their sources of information, choosing the most authoritative ones."

**In relationships:**
- Needs partner with clear logical framework
- Will follow if trust is established
- Struggles when expected to lead logical processing
- Good with 1L partners who provide structure

## Part II: Emotion Function (E)

**Domain:** Feelings, atmosphere, relationships, expression, aesthetics

### 1E — Primary Emotional Shaping

**Properties:** Resultative, Principled, Subjective

**Latent process:** "Quickly identifies emotional dynamic → assembles emotional frame → leads emotional climate"

**Real-world example (after a quarrel):**
- **Analysis:** "Now the problem is not in facts but in coldness and alienation"
- **Synthesis:** "Need to first return warmth, then discuss"
- Person assembles emotional frame of situation themselves

**From The Typendium:**
> "The first Emotion is strong, aggressive, monologue. This function is characterised by an absolute self-assurance in all aspects related to the particular aspect."

**In relationships:**
- Naturally senses emotional undercurrents
- May lead relationship emotional climate
- Comfortable expressing emotions and setting tone
- May not understand why others miss obvious dynamics

### 2E — Cooperative Emotional Shaping

**Properties:** Processive, Flexible, Subjective

**Latent process:** "Jointly identifies dynamics → synthesizes emotionally for both → adapts"

**Real-world example:**
- **Analysis:** "You're closing off, I'm nervous, so first need to reduce tension"
- **Synthesis:** "Let me say it softer, and you answer without defense—conversation will work"
- Emotional synthesis is cooperative

**In relationships:**
- Natural co-regulator of relationship emotional climate
- Values collaborative emotional processing
- Adapts emotional approach to partner's needs
- Creates safe space for both partners' emotions

### 3E — Anxious Emotional Mapping

**Properties:** Processive, Principled, Objective

**Latent process:** "Uncertain what feeling → difficulty expressing → assembles emotional whole with difficulty"

**Real-world example:**
- **Analysis:** "I don't understand what I'm feeling now and how to express it correctly. What if they misunderstand me?"
- **Synthesis:** Emotional whole assembled with difficulty, lots of constraint and self-control

**From bestsocionics.com:**
> "3E: 'Insecure, weak, conscious, processional, introverted. This function is associated with significant insecurity, pain, and doubt. Highly sensitive to criticism.'"

**In relationships:**
- May need significant support in emotional communication
- May suppress emotions until overflow
- Partner may need to help decode and create safe space
- Can appear emotionally distant even when feeling deeply

### 4E — Frame-Taking

**Properties:** Resultative, Unprincipled, Objective

**Latent process:** "Minimal emotional analysis → adapts to tone set by others → enters emotional space"

**Real-world example:**
- **Analysis:** "Please, no extra drama"
- **Synthesis:** "If you set a calm tone, I'll enter into it"
- Doesn't want to be primary emotional architect

**From bestsocionics.com:**
> "Fourth Emotion individuals are surprisingly calm in critical situations. That is, they think rationally, act and understand the situation. They are very stress-resistant."

**In relationships:**
- Needs partner with emotional clarity
- May struggle if expected to lead emotional processing
- Responds well to calm, clear emotional framing
- Can seem emotionally detached but is actually adapting

## Part III: Will/Volition Function (V)

**Domain:** Goals, decisions, control, initiative, responsibility

*Note: The term "volition" is used to distinguish from moral "willpower"*

### 1V — Primary Volitional Assembly

**Properties:** Resultative, Principled, Subjective

**Latent process:** "Quickly decomposes decision factors → self-assembles volitional plan → takes initiative"

**Real-world example (relocation decision):**
- **Analysis:** "There are 4 factors: work, money, family, deadlines. Work is most important, deadline is hard"
- **Synthesis:** "Plan is: in 2 months move, you close housing, I close work"
- Person assembles volitional construction of action themselves

**From The Typendium (quoting Afanasyev):**
> "Tolstoy had the following order of functions: 1st Will, 2nd Emotion, 3rd Physics, 4th Logic."

**In relationships:**
- Naturally takes initiative in decisions
- May need to watch for taking too much volitional control
- Comfortable with commitment and responsibility
- Good with partners who appreciate clear direction

### 2V — Cooperative Volitional Planning

**Properties:** Processive, Flexible, Subjective

**Latent process:** "Jointly explores fears/priorities → together assembles course → flexible about initiative"

**Real-world example:**
- **Analysis:** "Let's understand what scares you most about moving, and what's critical for me"
- **Synthesis:** "OK, then the general course is: first a trial period for 3 months, then a decision"
- Volitional synthesis done jointly

**In relationships:**
- Natural co-decision makers
- Values joint commitment process
- Shares initiative naturally with partner
- Good with any type that values collaboration

### 3V — Anxious Volitional Assembly

**Properties:** Processive, Principled, Objective

**Latent process:** "Stuck in competing pressures → uncertain about rights → difficulty assembling stable line"

**Real-world example:**
- **Analysis:** "If I give in—they'll push me. If I press—there will be conflict. What if I don't even have the right to decide?"
- **Synthesis:** Person finds it difficult to assemble stable line of action because topic of will is painful

**From bestsocionics.com:**
> "1V and 3V are focused on achieving their own goals, realizing their desires. They seek help and support for achieving their goals or supporting others' ambitions."

**In relationships:**
- May need support in asserting needs
- May appear passive or aggressive inconsistently
- Partner may need to create safe space for volitional expression
- Can struggle with commitment decisions

### 4V — Frame-Taking

**Properties:** Resultative, Unprincipled, Objective

**Latent process:** "Minimal independent analysis → follows provided course → calm when direction given"

**Real-world example:**
- **Analysis:** "What options do we have?"
- **Synthesis:** "If you've already thought through the route, I can calmly join"
- Doesn't like being main initiator

**From bestsocionics.com (accentuations of 4V):**
> "Three main behavior patterns for types with 4V:
> 1) The owner of 4V goes with the flow, does not make independent decisions and is subject to any external influences. Unsure of himself, weak-willed.
> 2) Strongly subject to his own desires, does what comes into his head, not prone to self-criticism.
> 3) Provides support to others, is actively interested in their goals and desires, strives for connection."

**In relationships:**
- May need partner with clear volitional direction
- Will commit strongly once plan is clear
- Can be "infected" with partner's dreams through prolonged exposure
- Good with 1V partners who provide structure

## Part IV: Physics Function (F)

**Domain:** Material world, comfort, resources, money, household, routine

### 1F — Primary Material Organization

**Properties:** Resultative, Principled, Subjective

**Latent process:** "Quickly sees material structure → self-assembles household/financial system → sets standards"

**Real-world example (family budget):**
- **Analysis:** "Here are mandatory expenses, here's the reserve, here's the nonsense we shouldn't spend on"
- **Synthesis:** "The financial scheme of the family should be like this"
- Natural material organizer

**In relationships:**
- May naturally manage household logistics
- May take lead on financial planning
- Comfortable setting material standards
- Should check if partner wants input

### 2F — Cooperative Material Organization

**Properties:** Processive, Flexible, Subjective

**Latent process:** "Jointly explores needs → assembles balanced system → flexible arrangements"

**Real-world example:**
- **Analysis:** "Let's look at what you really need for comfort, and what I need"
- **Synthesis:** "Let's assemble such an expense routine that it's comfortable for both"
- Creates shared material framework

**In relationships:**
- Natural co-manager of household and finances
- Values collaborative material decisions
- Adapts arrangements to partner's comfort
- Good with any type that values joint management

### 3F — Anxious Material Processing

**Properties:** Processive, Principled, Objective

**Latent process:** "Worried about adequacy → oscillates between extremes → difficulty establishing stable routine"

**Real-world example:**
- **Analysis:** "Are we really enough? Won't it look too poor? Am I too demanding?"
- **Synthesis:** Difficult to assemble stable household solution because material topic causes anxiety

**From bestsocionics.com:**
> "1F and 4F do not like to pay attention to their appearance and the appearance of other people. For them, the result matters, not the process of achieving it."

**In relationships:**
- May need reassurance about material decisions
- May fluctuate in material expectations
- Partner may need to help establish stable framework
- Can feel judged by partner's material standards

### 4F — Frame-Taking

**Properties:** Resultative, Unprincipled, Objective

**Latent process:** "Minimal independent analysis → adapts to established system → acts within framework"

**Real-world example:**
- **Analysis:** "Tell me what's the basic normal option"
- **Synthesis:** "OK, if this is a working household scheme, I'll adapt"
- Doesn't want to be primary material architect

**From bestsocionics.com:**
> "Fourth Physics in critical situations become indifferent to the physical world—they don't eat, sleep, shower, etc. However, for some of them, this is also typical in ordinary life."

**In relationships:**
- May need partner with clear material vision
- Will integrate well into established systems
- May struggle if expected to lead material organization
- Good with 1F partners who provide structure

## Function Properties Explained

From bestsocionics.com (function properties article):

### Subjective vs Objective

**Subjective functions (1, 2):**
- Can and want to influence others through this function
- Not subject to influence themselves in this area
- Have clear understanding of own/others' needs in this aspect

**Objective functions (3, 4):**
- Cannot influence others but are influenced by them
- Can be imposed upon by others' decisions
- "Easily made to do things by others"

### Conscious vs Unconscious

**Conscious functions (2, 3):**
- Person constantly focused on these aspects
- Notices slightest changes in this domain
- Significant attention devoted to processing

**Unconscious functions (1, 4):**
- Person unaware of own and others' states in this aspect
- Wants to achieve stable state to not return to these aspects
- Result-oriented: just want it to work

### Introverted vs Extraverted

**Introverted functions (1, 3):**
- Directed toward own state
- Focused on maintenance or change of own state
- Aggressive: pushes own approach

**Extraverted functions (2, 4):**
- Primarily interested in another person's state
- Focused on changing/supporting others' state
- Passive: adapts to others' approach

## Operational Compatibility Patterns

### Good Combinations

**1 + 4 in same aspect:**
- One assembles, other accepts without interference
- Examples: 1L + 4L, 1V + 4V, 1F + 4F
- Natural division of labor

**2 + 3 in same aspect:**
- One helps parse and assemble without pressure
- 2L helps 3L reach conclusions without overwhelming
- 2V helps 3V assert needs safely
- Creates supportive learning environment

**2 + 2:**
- Both naturally co-process
- Natural collaboration on all operational matters
- Flexible division of roles

### Challenging Combinations

**1 + 1:**
- Both want to be the primary scheme-builder
- Competition for logical/emotional/volitional leadership
- May argue about whose framework is correct

**1 + 3:**
- One already assembled, other still in analysis
- 1 may push 3 to conclude before ready
- 3 may feel pressured by 1's speed
- Risk of 1 dismissing 3's concerns

**3 + 3:**
- Both stuck in analysis
- May reinforce each other's doubts
- Difficulty reaching conclusions without external help
- Can spiral into indecision

**4 + 4:**
- Both wait for external framework
- May not generate necessary structure
- Struggle when no external authority provided
- May seem passive or helpless

### Cross-Function Conflicts

**Logic-Will conflicts:**
- 1L + 3V: "I see the optimal plan" vs. "I can't commit to any plan"
- Logical efficiency vs. volitional hesitation

**Emotion-Physics conflicts:**
- 1E + 3F: "Let's focus on feelings" vs. "Let's focus on practical matters"
- Emotional vs. material priorities
