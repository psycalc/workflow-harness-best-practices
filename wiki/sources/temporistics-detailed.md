# Temporistics: Strategic Level — Comprehensive Analysis

> **Level:** Strategic  
> **Latent Process:** Inductive-deductive structuring of temporal experience  
> **Frame Type:** Temporal frame  
> **Core Question:** "Where am I going and why?"

Temporistics is a typology describing how a person relates to time: past, present, future, and eternity. In the primary source set in this repo, the theory is associated with Dr. Radut / Nika Sherman and Alexander Latyshev, with the site name and much published material credited to Latyshev.

**Key distinction from Psychosophy:** In this project's framing, Psychosophy deals with attitudinal differences across logic, emotion, will, and physics, while Temporistics deals with differences in temporal worldview. The structural parallel to Psychosophy is explicit in the source history, but many detailed latent-process labels below are this repo's schema overlay rather than canonical Temporistics terminology.

---

## Theoretical Foundation

### The Four Temporal Aspects

| Aspect | Question | Domain |
|--------|----------|--------|
| **Past (P)** | "Who am I?" | Self-concept, identity, experience, causality, history |
| **Present (N)** | "Where is my place?" | Immediate environment, short-term events, involvement, spatial orientation |
| **Future (F)** | "Where am I going?" | Goals, planning, strategy, trajectory, potential |
| **Eternity (E)** | "Why am I?" | Meaning, purpose, existential continuity, observer position |

**Notation:**
- Russian: P-N-B-V (Прошлое, Настоящее, Будущее, Вечность)
- English: P-N-F-E (Past, Now/Present, Future, Eternity)

### The Four Positions

The position names are canonical. The latent-process labels in the table below are this repo's interpretive overlay.

| Position | Name | Latent Process | Characteristic Phrase |
|----------|------|---------------|---------------------|
| 1st | **Target** (Целевой) | Autonomous frame generation | "I set the frame myself" |
| 2nd | **Creative** (Творческий) | Cooperative frame development | "I develop the frame in dialogue" |
| 3rd | **Painful** (Болевой) | Reconstruction under deficit | "I agonizingly derive frame from problems" |
| 4th | **Blind** (Слепой) | External frame acceptance | "I take the frame from another" |

## Part I: The Past Aspect

**Core Question:** "Who am I?"

The Past aspect describes how a person relates to their history, how they interpret accumulated experience, and how their sense of identity is formed through temporal continuity.

From lore at temporistics.ru: *"The Past is one of the most captivating topics in our personal philosophy. Our relationship with past is deeply connected with our self-identification."*

### 1st Past — The Author (Автор)

**Alternative names:** Creator, Chronicler (in 2nd position)

**Latent process:** Person first holds an internal thesis about themselves, then reads the past through it. The general conclusion about self precedes specific memories.

**Deductive movement:** Self-concept → past facts confirm it

**From temporistics.ru:**
> "For the Author, the past is a confirmation of their identity. They tend to see their history as evidence of who they already know themselves to be."

**Real-world manifestation:**
- Selects past events that confirm established identity
- Setbacks reframed as character demonstrations
- "I am a person who always rises after defeats"
- Past is evidence, not source of identity

**Forum discussions (PDB Wiki):**
> "The Author sees their past through the lens of their present self-understanding. This can make them appear confident about their identity but potentially resistant to updating self-concept based on new experiences."

**In relationships:**
- Has stable narrative about own history
- May interpret partner's past through fixed lens
- Values partners who affirm their established identity
- May struggle to see partner's growth/change over time

**Compatibility patterns:**
- Good with 4th Past (Reader) who accepts their narrative
- Challenging with 3rd Past (Critic) who derives identity from painful past
- Both 1st Pasts may compete over whose history narrative is correct

### 2nd Past — The Chronicler (Летописец)

**Latent process:** Person flexibly processes experience through dialogue and comparison with others. Identity emerges through mutual reflection.

**Characteristic phrase:** "Let's look at your experience and mine, compare, understand what follows from this."

**From lore:**
> "The Chronicler processes experience jointly with others. They are less attached to a fixed self-narrative and more exploratory in how they understand their history."

**Real-world manifestation:**
- Values shared memories and mutual storytelling
- Updates self-understanding through relationship
- Comfortable with multiple perspectives on same events
- Identity is relational, not fixed

**In relationships:**
- Natural co-processor of shared history
- Enjoys reminiscing together
- Can help partner update their self-narrative
- May struggle if partner has very rigid past-identity connection

### 3rd Past — The Critic (Критик)

**Alternative names:** Judge, Painful Past

**Latent process:** Person collects particular experiences, especially painful ones, and derives heavy conclusions about self. Specific instances precede harsh generalizations.

**Inductive movement:** Painful past episodes → harsh conclusion about self

**From source tradition:**
> "The third aspect is always an area of solitude and darkness. In the case of the third Past, this is a critical, judging position toward one's history and self-identification."

**Forum discussions (Socionics forums):**
> "3P types often feel like their past 'proves' something negative about them. They collect evidence of failure, silence, weakness—and conclude they are fundamentally flawed."

**Real-world manifestation:**
- "Then I was silent. Then I stepped back again. Then I didn't push an important decision. Probably, I'm weak and break at critical moments."
- May have difficulty seeing positive past events
- Over-generalizes from specific failures
- Past often experienced as source of self-criticism

**Note:** Some community discussions mix third-Past and third-Eternity material. In this page they should be kept separate.

**In relationships:**
- May carry significant past relationship wounds
- Interprets current through lens of past failures
- Needs partner who can help reframe without minimizing
- May push away partners who remind them of painful past

**Compatibility patterns:**
- Needs 2nd Past partner who can co-process without judgment
- May clash with 1st Past who doesn't understand weight of past
- Good with partners who hold positive mirror

### 4th Past — The Reader (Читатель)

**Latent process:** Person less wants to build strong self-narrative and more easily takes ready frame from others.

**Characteristic phrase:** "Tell me how it was and what's important to remember."

**From lore:**
> "The Reader is not invested in constructing a comprehensive self-narrative. They can accept others' interpretation of shared history."

**Real-world manifestation:**
- Less concerned with historical self-interpretation
- Accepts family/community version of history
- Doesn't need to process past extensively
- Can move forward without deep past processing

**In relationships:**
- May look to partner for interpretation of shared history
- Less likely to initiate deep historical discussions
- Adapts to partner's narrative framing
- May seem to have less "history" than other types

## Part II: The Present Aspect

**Core Question:** "Where is my place?"

The Present aspect describes how a person perceives and acts within their immediate environment, how they understand their place in space and current situation.

### 1st Present — The Master (Хозяин)

**Alternative names:** Host, Owner

**Latent process:** Person first has internal sense of their right place, then acts in space from that conviction.

**Characteristic phrase:** "This is my territory. I understand how things should be arranged here."

**From temporistics.ru:**
> "The Master knows how to be happy in the moment, their motto is 'Here and Now.' The Master is a hedonist, which is connected with the first Present."

**Real-world manifestation:**
- Clear sense of territorial rights
- Tendency to organize chaotic environments
- Feels authorized to set frame for immediate situation
- "If there's chaos here, it needs to be put in order"

**In relationships:**
- May naturally take leadership in shared spaces
- Unsettled when sense of place is challenged
- Creates comfortable environments for self and others
- May need to watch for territorial rigidity

### 2nd Present — The Local (Местный)

**Latent process:** Person integrates into space through dialogue and mutual adaptation. Frame is developed cooperatively.

**Characteristic phrase:** "Let's together understand how to arrange things here and what will be comfortable for us."

**Real-world manifestation:**
- Flexible, adaptive to others' spatial needs
- Creates shared space through negotiation
- Less territorial pressure
- "I fit in easily wherever I am"

**In relationships:**
- Natural co-creator of shared domestic/social space
- Flexible about territory
- Adapts living space to partner's needs
- Comfortable with moving/relocating

### 3rd Present — The Exile (Изгнанник)

**Latent process:** Person collects environment signals and derives their position—often with uncertainty about where they fit.

**Characteristic phrase:** "Here everything seems already distributed. I don't understand where my place is."

**From temporistics.ru:**
> "The third Present is the exile—they feel not fully integrated into their environment."

**Real-world manifestation:**
- Reads the room extensively before acting
- May feel like outsider or not fully included
- Uncertainty about their position in groups
- Reactive to environmental cues

**In relationships:**
- Needs reassurance about place in partner's life
- Struggles with assertiveness in shared spaces
- May appear overly accommodating
- Needs explicit invitation into spaces/activities

### 4th Present — The Guest (Гость)

**Latent process:** Person takes orientation for immediate situation from others. Less internal pressure about place.

**Characteristic phrase:** "Show where to sit, where to go, how it's done here."

**Real-world manifestation:**
- Minimal own pressure on topic of place
- Willing to follow others' lead in immediate situation
- Adaptive rather than territorial
- Comfortable as visitor/ newcomer

**In relationships:**
- May need clear communication about role in shared activities
- Comfortable following partner's lead in unfamiliar situations
- Doesn't need own "territory"
- Adaptable to partner's living space preferences

## Part III: The Future Aspect

**Core Question:** "Where am I going?"

The Future aspect describes how a person relates to what's coming: goal formation, trajectory projection, uncertainty management, and planning.

### 1st Future — The Captain (Капитан)

**Latent process:** Person first holds clear image of trajectory, then derives actions from it. Destination precedes steps.

**Characteristic phrase:** "It's clear to me where I'm going. So today I need to refuse the unnecessary, this path is right, and that one is not."

**From lore:**
> "The Captain sees the dynamics of change and is able to plan a route to achieve goals in the future."

**Real-world manifestation:**
- Clear vision of relationship/life trajectory
- Willing to make present sacrifices for future goals
- "I know where we're going, trust the plan"
- Future is determined, not uncertain

**From PDB:**
> "The Captain can plan a route to achieve goals. They see the destination and work backwards to determine steps."

**In relationships:**
- May have clear vision of relationship trajectory
- Expects partner to align with projected future
- Can make long-term commitments confidently
- May struggle if partner is less future-oriented

### 2nd Future — The Helmsman (Рулевой)

**Latent process:** Person develops trajectory through dialogue and exploration with others. Future is navigated collaboratively.

**Characteristic phrase:** "Let's consider options and choose a route together."

**From lore:**
> "The Helmsman steers life's course but does not determine the destination. They adjust the path based on conditions."

**Real-world manifestation:**
- Flexible about specific destination
- Values joint decision-making about direction
- "We'll figure it out as we go"
- Future is a shared project

**In relationships:**
- Natural partner for long-term planning
- Values joint vision of future
- Adjusts plans based on partner's input
- Comfortable with uncertainty in trajectory

### 3rd Future — The Free Rider (Безбилетник)

**Latent process:** Person collects risks, uncertainties, failures and makes anxious conclusions about trajectory.

**Characteristic phrase:** "The economy is unstable, people are unpredictable, I've already made wrong choices, and I'm still not confident in myself. It seems I might go wrong again."

**From lore:**
> "The Free Rider is not responsible for planning the future and goes with the flow—but often anxiously."

**Real-world manifestation:**
- Future often imagined through potential problems
- Difficulty maintaining positive future image
- May catastrophize possible outcomes
- Focus on what could go wrong

**In relationships:**
- May need support maintaining positive relationship vision
- May express anxiety about future trajectory
- Can be paralyzed by fear of wrong choices
- Needs partner who can hold positive future image

### 4th Future — The Passenger (Пассажир)

**Latent process:** Person more easily takes future direction from others or external sources. Less internal drive to project trajectory.

**Characteristic phrase:** "If there's a reliable person or clear course, I'll calmly follow it."

**From lore:**
> "The Passenger leaves the future mostly to external forces outside their control."

**Real-world manifestation:**
- Future easier taken from outside than formulated from inside
- Willing to defer to trusted others
- Less anxious when direction is provided
- "You decide where we're going"

**In relationships:**
- May need partner with strong future vision
- Comfortable when direction is clear
- Follows partner's lead on major decisions
- May seem passive about future planning

## Part IV: The Eternity Aspect

**Core Question:** "Why am I?"

The Eternity aspect describes how a person relates to questions of meaning, purpose, and existential continuity beyond the immediate temporal frame. This is perhaps the most abstract aspect, dealing with the "big picture" of life purpose.

### 1st Eternity — The Guru (Гуру)

**Latent process:** Person starts from big meaning, principle, or frame, and reads life through it.

**Characteristic phrase:** "Life is not random. Everything has a purpose. So my trials also have a place in the general picture."

**From PDB Wiki:**
> "For the Guru, Eternity is a domain of self-actualization. They have the ability to impart meaning to both their own life and the world around them. Imparting meaning is distinct from searching for it."

**Real-world manifestation:**
- Strong existential framework
- Can create meaning when it isn't apparent
- Life seen as having structure and purpose
- "My suffering has meaning in the larger picture"

**From temporistics.ru:**
> "The Guru creates their own ideology, their own faith, their own principles and engages in their spreading."

**In relationships:**
- May have strong views on relationship meaning and purpose
- Sees relationship as part of larger life meaning
- Can help partner find meaning in difficulties
- May impose meaning framework on resistant partner

### 2nd Eternity — The Philosopher (Философ)

**Latent process:** Person develops existential framework through dialogue, comparison of worldviews, joint philosophizing.

**Characteristic phrase:** "Let's discuss, compare worldviews, search for meaning together."

**From PDB Wiki:**
> "Individuals with a creative eternity are marked by strong analytical skills, an ability to perceive connections between events, and a detached perspective. Unlike the first Eternity, which often creates or synthesizes meaning when it is not immediately apparent, the second does not find this..."

**Real-world manifestation:**
- Open to others' perspectives on existential questions
- Meaning emerges through relationship and dialogue
- Less attached to fixed metaphysical framework
- Enjoys philosophical discussions

**In relationships:**
- Natural partner for deep philosophical discussions
- Values shared existential exploration
- Can hold space for partner's meaning questions
- May seem abstract or detached from practical concerns

### 3rd Eternity — The Philistine/Common Man (Обыватель)

**Alternative names:** Layman, Common Person

**Latent process:** Person tries to derive meaning from fragments of experience, pain, injustice—but derivation is anxious and unstable.

**Characteristic phrase:** "People suffer, die, make mistakes, much is meaningless. Then what is the meaning of my life specifically? Does it even exist?"

**From PDB Wiki:**
> "For those with a vulnerable Eternity, the questions of meaning are the most critical. Meaningfulness is an ongoing struggle."

**From temporistics.ru:**
> "The third Eternity... may feel the absence of global meaning in their life. This is an area of solitude and questioning."

**Real-world manifestation:**
- Deep existential questions without stable answers
- May see mostly meaninglessness and suffering
- Difficulty integrating pain into coherent meaning
- Gap between existential questions and available answers

**Forum discussions:**
> "3E types often struggle with 'why am I here' questions. They see suffering and injustice and can't reconcile it with a purposeful universe."

**In relationships:**
- May struggle with meaning questions in relationship context
- Needs partner who can hold meaning without forcing premature closure
- May appear existential or melancholic
- Can help partner confront difficult life questions honestly

### 4th Eternity — The Student (Ученик)

**Latent process:** Person more easily accepts existing meaning systems, traditions, or teachers rather than creating metaphysics independently.

**Characteristic phrase:** "It's easier for me to rely on an already existing system of meaning, tradition, teacher, faith."

**From PDB Wiki:**
> "For the Disciple, questions of meaning might be intriguing, but they rank at the bottom in their hierarchy of temporal values. Essentially, they forgo the pursuit of meaning to allocate time and energy to other, more practical, 'real-life' tasks."

**Real-world manifestation:**
- Not so much create metaphysics yourself, as accept and learn
- Stable existential framework through external authority
- Less existential anxiety when framework is provided
- "I follow the tradition/teacher/faith"

**In relationships:**
- May need partner with clear value/meaning framework
- Comfortable when relational meaning is provided by tradition or shared faith
- Not interested in debating existential questions endlessly
- Values practical meaning (what gives joy/purpose in daily life)

## Tetra Classifications

Temporistics groups types into six tetrads based on the two "worlds" described in the source materials:

**Face world:** Present and Future  
**Reverse world:** Eternity and Past

| Tetrad | Aspect Pair | Types |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| **Antipodes** | N + P | Politician, Player, Maestro, Game Master |
| **Guardians and Border Guards** | E + P | Missionary, Standard-Bearer, Rescuer, Knight |
| **Old-Timers and Founders of the Face World** | N + E | Theorist, Oracle, Conqueror, Star |
| **Conductors** | F + E | Ideologue, Samurai, Colonist, Pioneer |
| **Scouts and Reconnaissance Types** | N + F | Sommelier, Grey Cardinal, Hacker, Scout |
| **Nomads and Wanderers** | P + F | Initiator, Robinson, Pathfinder, Tamada |

## Internet Research Summary

### Forum Perspectives

From Personality Database Wiki discussions:
- Temporistics is considered a "worldview typology" — it describes what people consider important, not just how they process information
- The system has 24 types (4! permutations of aspect priority)
- Each type is written as 4 letters, e.g., "EPNF" meaning Eternity=1st, Past=2nd, Present=3rd, Future=4th

### Common Confusions (from forums)

1. **Confusing with Psychosophy:** People often mix up the aspect meanings. Remember: Psychosophy is about processing style (HOW), Temporistics is about temporal values (WHAT MATTERS).

2. **Confusing with MBTI:** The systems are fundamentally different. MBTI focuses on cognition, while Temporistics focuses on temporal values and worldview.

3. **3rd position confusion:** The "painful" aspect is not simply about suffering; it is the position where deficits and vulnerability are most noticeable.

### Real User Experiences (forums)

From Socionics forum discussions:
- Users report that knowing their Temporistics type helped explain relationship patterns
- The "past identity" dimension is often most striking in self-recognition
- Some find the Eternity aspect abstract and difficult to identify
