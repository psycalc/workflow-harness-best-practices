# Temporistics Eternity Position — Altruism Hypothesis

> **Status:** Hypothesis for testing — This document records a user-originated hypothesis about whether Eternity positions in Temporistics correlate with social value orientation.

---

## Hypothesized Pattern

This page proposes a possible relationship between Eternity position (1E-4E) in Temporistics and social orientation:

| Position | Type Name | Reasoning Direction | Predicted Orientation |
|-----------|----------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **1E** | Guru | Deductive | Altruist / Collectivist |
| **2E** | Philosopher | Inductive | Individualist |
| **3E** | Philistine | Inductive | Individualist |
| **4E** | Student | Deductive | Altruist / Collectivist |

**Notation:** Use **E** for Eternity (not V). Example: `EPNF` = Eternity > Past > Now > Future.

**Important caveat:** No published Temporistics source in the current project corpus directly links Eternity position to altruism, collectivism, allocentrism, or individualism.

---

## Rationale

### Deductive Positions (1E, 4E)

**Definition:** Reasoning from big meaning → to personal application

- **1E (Guru):** Creates their own meaning/ideology, then applies it to life
- **4E (Student):** Accepts externally-provided meaning, follows it

**Hypothesized mechanism:** Both start from an external/internal "big truth" (God, ideology, principle) and derive personal behavior from it. This may create a stronger connection to something larger than self.

**Hypothesized consequence:** Higher allocentrism or collectivist orientation.

---

### Inductive Positions (2E, 3E)

**Definition:** Reasoning from personal experience → to general meaning

- **2E (Philosopher):** Seeks meaning through personal exploration
- **3E (Philistine):** Doubts, questions meaning through personal experience

**Hypothesized mechanism:** Both derive meaning from personal observation and experience. This may keep attention centered more on individual search than on externally given collective frames.

**Hypothesized consequence:** More idiocentric or individualist orientation.

---

## Supporting Evidence from General Research

### Time Orientation → Altruism

Research shows:

1. **Future orientation → prosocial behavior** (Van Lange et al., 2009)
   - Future-oriented individuals show more generosity in social dilemmas
   - They think about long-term consequences for the group

2. **Allocentrism / idiocentrism** (Triandis tradition)
   - Allocentrism refers to stronger orientation to group goals and in-group needs
   - Idiocentrism refers to stronger orientation to personal goals and autonomy
   - This is conceptually relevant, but it is not evidence about Temporistics positions specifically

3. **Intertemporal prosocial preferences** (Hong, 2023)
   - Future time perspective predicts intertemporal altruism
   - Individuals with high future orientation show less discounting of others' welfare

### The Link to Eternity

Eternity aspect in Temporistics answers "Why am I?" — questions of meaning, purpose, relationship to something larger. This is conceptually related to:

- **Allocentrism** — connection to group/collective
- **Social value orientation** — preference for collective vs personal outcomes
- **Meaning orientation** — relationship to purpose and worldview

---

## The Hypothesis

**Hypothesis:** Individuals with Eternity in the **first or fourth position** (1E, 4E) in Temporistics may display higher levels of allocentrism/collectivism than those with Eternity in the **second or third position** (2E, 3E).

**Mechanism:**
- Deductive reasoning (from meaning → to self) creates connection to external framework
- This framework provides "big picture" belonging, reducing self-focus
- Inductive reasoning (from self → to meaning) keeps focus on individual search

## Testing This Hypothesis

### Required Research

1. **Collect Temporistics types** for participants (1E-4E positions)
2. **Measure** allocentrism/idiocentrism scales:
   - Allocentrism scale (Triandis)
   - Social Value Orientation (Van Lange)
   - IPIP collectivism items
3. **Compare** 1E+4E groups vs 2E+3E groups

### Expected Results

| Group | n | Expected Allocentrism Score |
|-------|-----|--------------------------|
| 1E + 4E | ~ | Higher |
| 2E + 3E | ~ | Lower |

### Alternative Hypotheses

1. **Only 4E** predicts collectivism (blind acceptance)
2. **Only 1E** predicts altruism (creators, not followers)
3. **Position matters only when combined with other aspects** (e.g., 1E + high Future)
