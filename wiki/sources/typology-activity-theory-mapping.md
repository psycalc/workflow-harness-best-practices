# Typology to Activity Theory Level Mapping

> **Status:** Proposed interpretive mapping — This document records the current project heuristic for aligning three typologies with Activity Theory levels.

---

## The Core Question

**Q:** Is Socionics "tactical" and Psychosophy "operational" correct? Or should it be reversed?

**A:** Within this project, the most natural working interpretation is Socionics -> operation, Psychosophy -> action, and Temporistics -> activity. This should be treated as a heuristic, not as settled doctrine in the literature.

---

## Activity Theory — Leontiev's Hierarchy

From Leontiev (1978), as cited in Activity Theory literature:

| Level | Term | Question | Description |
|-------|------|----------|-------------|
| **Activity** | Activity | **"Why?"** | Oriented to motives — objects that impel by themselves. The "why" of behavior. |
| **Action** | Action | **"What?"** | Directed at specific conscious goals. The "what" — conscious objective. |
| **Operation** | Operation | **"How?"** | Determined by actual conditions. The "how" — methods of accomplishing. |

**Key quote from Leontiev (1978):**
> "I call the methods for accomplishing actions, operations."

**Key quote from Kaptelinin & Nardi (2006):**
> "Operations are the automatic, condition-dependent methods that subordinate to actions."

---

## Analysis: What Does Each Typology Describe?

### Socionics — Information Metabolism

**Definition:** "Information metabolism is how our thinking works... the way in which the psyche processes information."

**From socionics-wiki sources:**
> "Socionics describes information metabolism using the eight functions of Model A. Each function is tasked with working with a specific type of information, perceiving, processing and assimilating it in a unique way."

**Key properties:**
- Functions are **inborn patterns** of information processing
- They operate **automatically** — not conscious goals
- They describe **how** information is perceived and processed
- From Bukalov: functions are "crystallised operations"

**Project interpretation:** Socionics is the closest fit to **"how"** and can be modeled as analogous to **operation** within this project's framework.

---

### Psychosophy — Function Influence

**Definition (from "Syntax of Love"):**
> "Psychosophy is the system of interaction between people through the transfer of their aspect states."

**The four aspects:**
- **Volition** (Воля) — intentions, desires, goals
- **Emotion** (Эмоция) — feelings, sentiments
- **Logic** (Логика) — opinions, views
- **Physics** (Физика) — physical needs, conditions

**From source:**
> "The aspect of volition determines the extent to which a person is willing to go to achieve their goals, and dreams. It is the confidence in own existence and abilities, the will to go further."

**Key properties:**
- Describes **what** domains a person engages with (volition, emotion, logic, physics)
- Deals with **conscious intentions and goals**
- Function position determines **role distribution** and **goal-setting**

**Project interpretation:** Psychosophy can be treated as closer to **"what"** a person consciously prioritizes or tries to enact, making it a plausible analogy to **action**.

---

### Temporistics — Time Relationship to Values

**Definition:**
> "Temporistics describes how a person relates to time: past, present, future, and eternity as value-orienting categories."

**Key properties:**
- Relationship to **Past/Present/Future/Eternity**
- **Value orientation** — what person considers meaningful
- Strategic worldview — "confidence in tomorrow"

**Project interpretation:** Temporistics is the closest fit to questions of value orientation and motive, making it a plausible analogy to **activity**.

## Working Mapping Table

| Activity Theory Level | Question | Typology | What It Measures |
|---------------------|----------|---------|----------------|
| **Activity** (мотив) | "Why?" — ценности, мотивация | **Temporistics** | Отношение ко времени как ценностная ориентация |
| **Action** (цель) | "What?" — сознательные цели | **Psychosophy** | Области приложения усилий: воля, эмоции, логика, физика |
| **Operation** (операция) | "How?" — автоматическое исполнение | **Socionics** | Информационный метаболизм — как автоматически обрабатываешь инфу |

---

## Reasoning Chain

### For Socionics → Operation/Tactical

1. **Leontiev (1978):** "I call the methods for accomplishing actions, operations."
2. **Bukalov:** Functions are "crystallised operations" — inborn patterns
3. **Socionics source:** "Information metabolism is how our thinking works" — automatic processing
4. **Kaptelinin (2006):** "Operations are the automatic, condition-dependent methods"

**Therefore within this project's heuristic:** Socionics is the closest of the three systems to operation-level, condition-dependent processing.

### For Psychosophy → Action/Operational

1. **Leontiev:** Actions are "directed at specific conscious goals"
2. **Afanasyev:** Psychosophy aspects = "volition, emotion, logic, physics" = domains of **intention and goal**
3. **Source:** "The aspect of volition determines the extent to which a person is willing to go to achieve their goals"

**Therefore within this project's heuristic:** Psychosophy is the closest of the three systems to goal-directed action-level framing.

---

## Why Alternative Mappings Fit Worse In This Framework

**If we reversed them:**

| If Socionics were "Operational" | If Psychosophy were "Tactical" |
|---------------------------|---------------------------|
| Then Socionics would describe "conscious goals" | Then Psychosophy would describe "automatic methods" |
| But Socionics functions are **inborn, automatic** | But Psychosophy functions are **conscious attitudes** |
| Fits the project heuristic less well | Fits the project heuristic less well |

**Under this interpretation, the alternative fit is weaker because:**
- Socionics functions are NOT conscious goals — they are automatic processing
- Psychosophy aspects are NOT automatic methods — they are conscious intentions

## Working Verdict

**Current project mapping:**

```
Activity Theory Level     →     Typology          →    Layer in Our System
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Activity (Why)         →   Temporistics     →    Strategic
Action (What)         →   Psychosophy      →    Operational  
Operation (How)       →   Socionics       →    Tactical
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

**Socionics** = best fit for "how" information is processed → **Operation** → **Tactical**
**Psychosophy** = best fit for "what" domains a person consciously prioritizes → **Action** → **Operational**
**Temporistics** = best fit for "why" a worldview or value orientation is organized as it is → **Activity** → **Strategic**
