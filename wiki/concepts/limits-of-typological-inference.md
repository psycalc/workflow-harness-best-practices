# Limits of Typological Inference

## Summary

Typological inference in PsyCalc is limited. It can generate hypotheses about interaction style, communication, motivation, and possible friction points. It cannot directly infer moral character, spiritual maturity, family readiness, personal worth, or relational destiny.

**Biblical grounding for the summary:**

- **Matthew 7:16–20** — character is known by fruits.
- **1 Samuel 16:7** — outward indicators do not fully reveal the heart.
- **James 4:13–15** — humans cannot guarantee future outcomes.
- **Proverbs 11:14** — serious decisions require counsel.

## No inference from type to moral character

**Claim:** A type cannot prove whether a person is faithful, honest, humble, responsible, repentant, or capable of sacrificial love.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **Matthew 7:16–20** — character is known by fruits.
- **Luke 16:10** — faithfulness is shown in concrete small actions.
- **1 Samuel 16:7** — outward indicators do not reveal the heart fully.

## No inference from score to destiny

**Claim:** A compatibility score cannot determine whether two people should marry, whether God blesses a relationship, or whether the relationship will succeed.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **James 4:13–15** — humans cannot control or guarantee the future.
- **Proverbs 16:9** — a person plans, but outcomes are not fully in human hands.
- **Proverbs 19:20–21** — counsel matters, but human plans remain limited.

## No replacement for counsel and community

**Claim:** Typology must not replace prayer, pastoral guidance, trusted counsel, observation over time, and serious conversation.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **Proverbs 11:14** — safety is found in many counselors.
- **Proverbs 15:22** — plans are established by counsel.
- **James 1:5** — wisdom should be sought from God.

## No use as pressure or ranking

**Claim:** Typology must not be used to shame, rank, reject, spiritually pressure, or control people.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **James 2:1** — faith must not show partiality.
- **Romans 12:3** — think soberly, not arrogantly.
- **Philippians 2:3** — avoid conceit and selfish ambition.

## Editorial rule

When writing about typology in the wiki or site, use language such as:

- “may suggest”
- “hypothesis”
- “possible pattern”
- “requires conversation and validation”
- “does not determine”

Avoid language such as:

- “proves”
- “guarantees”
- “destined”
- “God’s chosen type”
- “spiritually compatible by type”
