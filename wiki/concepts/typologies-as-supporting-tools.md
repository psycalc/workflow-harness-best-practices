# Typologies as Supporting Tools

## Summary

In the Christian family-formation frame, typologies are supporting tools. They may help people notice and discuss differences in perception, communication, motivation, stress, time orientation, and conflict. They must not be treated as spiritual verdicts, destiny, moral rankings, or substitutes for wisdom and character.

**Biblical grounding for the summary:**

- **Romans 12:4–5** — people differ and yet belong in mutual relationship.
- **Matthew 7:16–20** — spiritual and moral evaluation is by fruits, not labels.
- **1 Corinthians 8:1** — knowledge can puff up if not governed by love.
- **James 1:5** — wisdom is needed for serious decisions.

## What typologies may help with

**Claim:** Typologies may provide a limited language for discussing recurring differences between people.

Examples:

- how a person reacts under stress;
- how a person prefers to reason, feel, plan, or act;
- where conflict may arise;
- what questions a couple should discuss before serious decisions.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **Romans 12:4–5** — people differ and yet belong in mutual relationship.
- **1 Corinthians 12:4–6** — gifts and workings differ, but difference does not define ultimate worth.
- **Proverbs 18:13** — wise understanding requires hearing before answering.

## What typologies cannot determine

**Claim:** Typologies cannot determine spiritual maturity, faithfulness, repentance, holiness, capacity to love, readiness for marriage, or God’s will for a specific relationship.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **Matthew 7:16–20** — people are known by fruits.
- **Galatians 5:22–23** — spiritual maturity is shown by the fruit of the Spirit.
- **1 Samuel 16:7** — the heart is not fully visible through outward indicators.

## Why the tool must stay humble

**Claim:** Typological knowledge can become harmful if it produces pride, fatalism, excuses, contempt, or avoidance of repentance and growth.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **1 Corinthians 8:1** — knowledge can puff up, but love builds up.
- **Philippians 2:3** — do nothing from rivalry or conceit.
- **Galatians 6:4–5** — each person must examine their own work and carry responsibility.
