# Family Formation Principles

## Summary

Family formation in this project is ordered by primary and secondary criteria. Primary criteria concern faith, values, character, fidelity, responsibility, forgiveness, and community context. Secondary criteria include personality, preferences, communication style, temperament, and typological compatibility.

**Biblical grounding for the summary:**

- **Matthew 7:24–25** — the foundation matters when pressure comes.
- **Galatians 5:22–23** — character and spiritual fruit are central.
- **Colossians 3:13–14** — forgiveness and love are essential for unity.
- **1 Corinthians 8:1** — knowledge must remain subordinate to love.

See [[christian-foundation-of-family]] for the worldview frame and [[typologies-as-supporting-tools]] for the role of typologies.

## Primary criteria

**Claim:** Shared faith and values matter more than surface attraction or typological fit because they shape the long-term direction of family life.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **Matthew 7:24–25** — the foundation determines whether the house stands under pressure.
- **Amos 3:3** — walking together requires agreement in direction.
- **2 Corinthians 6:14** — shared spiritual direction matters deeply in close covenantal bonds.

**Claim:** Fidelity and trust are central because family cannot mature where promises, truthfulness, and reliability are unstable.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **Proverbs 20:6** — faithful people are precious and not easy to find.
- **Luke 16:10** — faithfulness is shown in small things.
- **Ephesians 4:25** — truthfulness is required because people belong to one another.

**Claim:** Responsibility and service are primary because family requires daily self-giving, not only emotional intensity.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **Philippians 2:3–4** — love attends to the interests of the other.
- **Galatians 5:13** — freedom is for serving one another through love.
- **Ephesians 5:1–2** — love follows the pattern of self-giving.

**Claim:** Forgiveness and repair matter because every relationship faces failure, hurt, misunderstanding, and conflict.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **Colossians 3:13–14** — forgiveness and love hold community together.
- **Ephesians 4:31–32** — bitterness is to be replaced by kindness and forgiveness.
- **Matthew 18:15** — repair requires truthful confrontation, not denial.

## Secondary criteria

**Claim:** Temperament, communication style, and typological compatibility may influence ease of interaction, but they should remain secondary to faith, character, and covenantal responsibility.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **1 Corinthians 8:1** — knowledge can inflate if it is not governed by love.
- **Romans 12:3** — people should think soberly about themselves, not proudly.
- **Proverbs 18:13** — understanding requires listening before judging.

## Practical use in matching

PsyCalc-compatible matching should optimize for:

1. shared Christian seriousness and values;
2. readiness for honest conversation;
3. visible character and responsibility;
4. ability to repair conflict;
5. supportive community context;
6. only then typological and communication-fit hypotheses.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **Matthew 7:16–20** — look for fruits.
- **Proverbs 15:22** — plans benefit from counsel.
- **1 Thessalonians 5:21** — test claims and hold fast to what is good.

## Caveats

- High compatibility is not a guarantee.
- Low compatibility is not an automatic prohibition.
- A type does not reveal holiness, faithfulness, repentance, or capacity to love.
- Church involvement must not become social pressure or romantic coercion.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **James 4:13–15** — do not presume control over outcomes.
- **1 Samuel 16:7** — human beings cannot see the heart as God does.
