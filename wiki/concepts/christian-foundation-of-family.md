# Christian Foundation of Family

## Summary

PsyCalc / Cognitive Matchmaker is being reframed around the conviction that a strong family is built not only on feelings, attraction, or compatibility signals, but on shared values, fidelity, responsibility, forgiveness, mature love, and a supportive community. Christian faith gives this a deep foundation, while typologies remain only secondary tools for self-understanding and conversation.

**Biblical grounding for the summary:**

- **1 Corinthians 13:4–7** — love is patient, enduring, and not reducible to intensity.
- **Colossians 3:12–14** — forgiveness and love bind relationships together.
- **Matthew 7:24–25** — durable life requires a foundation.
- **1 Thessalonians 5:21** — human tools and claims should be tested.

See also [[family-formation-principles]], [[typologies-as-supporting-tools]], and [[research-layer-vs-practical-guidance]].

## Core thesis

**Claim:** A strong family requires more than romantic feeling; it needs shared values, fidelity, responsibility, forgiveness, and community support.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **1 Corinthians 13:4–7** — love is patient, faithful, enduring, and not reducible to emotional intensity.
- **Colossians 3:12–14** — mature relationships require compassion, humility, patience, forgiveness, and love.
- **Ecclesiastes 4:9–12** — mutual support and the “threefold cord” strengthen life together.

## Why Christian faith is primary here

**Claim:** Christian faith gives a deep foundation for family because it frames love as covenantal, responsible, forgiving, truthful, and self-giving rather than merely preference-based or emotionally driven.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **Matthew 7:24–25** — the stability of a house depends on its foundation.
- **Ephesians 5:1–2** — love is shaped by self-giving.
- **Philippians 2:3–4** — mature love seeks the good of the other, not only self-interest.

## Why typologies are secondary

**Claim:** Typologies can help people discuss differences in communication, perception, motivation, conflict, and stress, but they cannot define a person’s spiritual condition, moral character, or suitability for marriage.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **Matthew 7:16–20** — people are known by fruits, not abstract labels.
- **1 Samuel 16:7** — God sees the heart beyond outward markers.
- **Galatians 5:22–23** — maturity is recognized by the fruit of the Spirit, not by a profile.

## Pastoral limits

**Claim:** No test or model should replace prayer, pastoral counsel, wise community, real conversation, observation over time, or personal responsibility.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **James 1:5** — serious decisions require wisdom.
- **Proverbs 11:14** — there is safety in wise counsel.
- **1 Thessalonians 5:21** — all claims should be tested.

## Non-claims

This page does not claim that:

- Christian faith mechanically guarantees marriage or family success.
- A church community automatically produces safe relationships.
- Typology can identify “God’s chosen spouse.”
- Compatibility scores should overrule character, freedom, wisdom, or pastoral discernment.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **James 4:13–15** — humans should not presume control over future outcomes.
- **Proverbs 16:9** — people plan their way, but the final outcome is not theirs to control.
