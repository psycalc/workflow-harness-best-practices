# Temporistics Test Specification

This page defines the first PsyCalc draft test for Temporistics. It separates source-backed Temporistics structure from PsyCalc's interpretive overlay.

## Status

**Draft / ready for expert review, not validated.**

The test should produce a temporal-frame hypothesis, not a definitive statement about identity or destiny.

## Construct Frame

Source-backed Temporistics uses four aspects:

- **Past** — “Who am I?”
- **Present** — “Where is my place?”
- **Future** — “Where am I going?”
- **Eternity** — “Why am I?”

And four positions:

- **1 Target** — goal;
- **2 Creative** — instrument;
- **3 Painful** — criterion;
- **4 Blind** — resource.

PsyCalc interprets Temporistics as a heuristic model of **latent processes of induction and deduction in temporal/existential experience**.

## Primary Scales

Aspect scales:

| Aspect | Construct |
|---|---|
| Past | identity through history, memory, continuity, biography |
| Present | place, current belonging, situational embeddedness |
| Future | route, direction, planning horizon, trajectory |
| Eternity | meaning, ultimate frame, trans-situational purpose |

Position-mode scales:

| Position | Construct |
|---|---|
| Target | I define this temporal frame myself |
| Creative | I elaborate this frame flexibly and relationally |
| Painful | I treat failures here as painful criteria |
| Blind | I borrow or deprioritize this frame |

The ideal bank eventually scores 16 cells: `aspect × position`.

## Recommended MVP Structure

- 60–100 total items for a full pilot bank.
- Likert items for stable temporal tendencies.
- Forced-choice items to compare Past / Present / Future / Eternity salience.
- Scenario items for disruption, transition, project start, loss of meaning, relocation, and group conflict.
- Optional open-ended prompts for later qualitative coding.

Use a 6-point Likert scale without a neutral midpoint.

## Seed Item Bank

These are original seed items for review. They are not a validated test.

### Likert Items

1. After an important event, I first try to understand what it adds to the story of who I am. `Past`
2. When I enter a new environment, I quickly need to understand what place I can realistically occupy there. `Present`
3. Even if things are acceptable now, I find it hard to relax when I do not know where my path is leading. `Future`
4. If I cannot see a wider reason for what I am doing, my motivation weakens quickly. `Eternity`
5. I usually have an inner picture of who I am, and I interpret past episodes through that picture. `Past-Target`
6. I understand my place best through shared action and live feedback from the environment. `Present-Creative`
7. A few wrong turns in life can make me doubt whether I know how to choose a direction at all. `Future-Painful`
8. In questions of ultimate meaning, I often prefer to rely on an existing framework rather than build one from scratch. `Eternity-Blind`
9. I can move forward without resolving every question about my past. `Past lower salience / possible Blind`
10. I feel disoriented when I cannot sense my current role among people around me. `Present salience`
11. I often evaluate present choices by asking what long-term line they create. `Future`
12. I need my work and relationships to connect to a larger meaning, not only immediate usefulness. `Eternity`

### Forced-Choice Items

13. During life turbulence, what do you most need to restore first?
    - A. who I am now after what happened; `Past`
    - B. where I belong right now; `Present`
    - C. where I should go next; `Future`
    - D. why this still matters. `Eternity`

14. When helping someone after a major failure, I would first help them:
    - A. reconstruct the story and what it says about them; `Past`
    - B. regain their place in the current environment; `Present`
    - C. see a next route and possible directions; `Future`
    - D. find a meaning-frame that can hold the event. `Eternity`

15. Which discomfort is strongest?
    - A. my history no longer explains who I am; `Past-Painful`
    - B. I do not know where I fit; `Present-Painful`
    - C. my future route is broken; `Future-Painful`
    - D. I cannot find a larger reason. `Eternity-Painful`

16. Which support feels most useful from another person?
    - A. they help me remember and integrate what already happened; `Past-Creative support`
    - B. they help me orient in the current situation; `Present-Creative support`
    - C. they help me shape the next direction; `Future-Creative support`
    - D. they help me connect events to meaning. `Eternity-Creative support`

## Scoring Sketch

1. Score aspect salience for Past, Present, Future, Eternity.
2. Score position modes: Target, Creative, Painful, Blind.
3. Build a 16-cell heatmap for aspect-position signals.
4. Compare the heatmap with 24 temporal-frame permutations.
5. Return best-fit hypothesis, nearby alternatives, and uncertainty notes.

## Caveats

- Future can be confused with conscientious planning or anxiety.
- Present can be confused with social confidence or extraversion.
- Eternity can be confused with religiosity, ideology, depression, or philosophical vocabulary.
- Past can be confused with nostalgia, trauma, or autobiographical memory style.
- Position inference is harder than aspect salience.
- Do not frame results as spiritual authority, fate, or existential diagnosis.
