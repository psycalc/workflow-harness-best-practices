# Hypothesis Status of PsyCalc

## Summary

PsyCalc is a heuristic and research-oriented framework. Its typological mappings and compatibility interpretations are not Christian doctrine, not proven personality science, and not deterministic laws. They are structured hypotheses that should be tested, corrected, and limited by evidence, ethics, and practical wisdom.

**Biblical grounding for the summary:**

- **1 Thessalonians 5:21** — test everything and hold fast to what is good.
- **Proverbs 18:17** — initial claims require examination.
- **1 Corinthians 13:9** — human knowledge is partial.
- **Colossians 2:8** — human systems should not capture the person as final truth.

## Core hypothesis status

**Claim:** PsyCalc treats Socionics, Psychosophy, and Temporistics as possible hypothesis languages for latent psychological processes, not as final truths about the person.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **1 Thessalonians 5:21** — test everything and hold fast to what is good.
- **Proverbs 18:17** — first claims can sound right until examined.
- **1 Corinthians 13:9** — human knowledge is partial.

## Compatibility status

**Claim:** Compatibility estimates are provisional and should be interpreted as prompts for conversation and discernment, not as decisions.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **Proverbs 15:22** — plans require counsel.
- **James 1:5** — wisdom is needed for decisions.
- **James 4:13–15** — outcomes cannot be guaranteed.

## Relationship to Christian guidance

**Claim:** Christian moral and pastoral categories are primary for family formation; PsyCalc models are secondary and instrumental.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **Matthew 7:16–20** — evaluate by fruits.
- **Galatians 5:22–23** — maturity is fruit, not a typological label.
- **Colossians 3:12–14** — love, humility, patience, and forgiveness are central.

## Validation direction

Future research should test:

- whether typological hypotheses improve conversations;
- whether they predict any outcomes beyond simpler baseline measures;
- whether users understand caveats;
- whether the tool reduces or increases pressure, judgment, or false certainty.

**Biblical grounding:**

- **Proverbs 21:2** — people may see their own ways as right, but deeper examination is needed.
- **1 Thessalonians 5:21** — testing is a virtue, not a threat.
