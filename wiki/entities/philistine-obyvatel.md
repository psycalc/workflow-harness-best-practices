# Philistine (Обыватель)

Key question of Eternity: "WHY?"

Eternity is otherworldliness, meaning, harmony, ideas, integrity.

## Characteristics

- Third aspect is always an area of loneliness and darkness
- May feel the absence of global meaning in their life
- At the worldview level, may have a certain duality—craving for "there" to be something and wanting to have a connection with it
- At the same time, believes that even if there is something or someone "there," they have no business to them, the small human being

## The Philistine's Strategy

The name "Philistine" was chosen because of the erroneous strategy that painful eternity bearers sometimes use to escape the pressing evaluation of their criterion. Constant doubts about the meaningfulness of their life can lead to a state of permanent melancholy, and they often find an outlet in hiding behind the mask of a philistine.

## Meaning as Criterion

For eternity in the painful aspect, questions of meaning are most critical. Meaningfulness is an important **criterion** of quality of life for such people. However, the pursuit of meaningfulness for the Philistine is expressed completely differently than for the Guru. If for the Guru, meaning is a positive, motivating aspect, then for the Philistine it is limiting, requiring "not to waste time on nonsense," directive and commanding.

## The Philistine's Strength

The Philistine (in the Temporistic sense) is closer to understanding their purpose, their calling, than all other types. They sense earlier and more subtly when they have deviated from the true path.

## Famous Examples

- Maria (ПНВБ) about a difficult period: "Then I started asking questions—why am I here? Then the brain-digging began... I fell into depression. Sometimes I had thoughts about suicide."
