# Guru (Гуру)

The Guru creates their own ideology, their own faith, their own principles, and engages in their distribution.

## Characteristics

- Eternity is the area of self-realization
- **Meaning** is the driving, motivating force of their existence
- In the sphere of meaning, the Guru is in the position of "I plus, you minus"
- Defines the meaning of things as their task only
- Has little doubt about their right to decide
- Possesses the strength and ability to give meaning to everything around them
- After watching a boring film, someone might simply write off that time as lost; the Guru will draw conclusions
- Constantly extracts meaning, morality, cannot stop even without sufficient information for reflection

## Key Emotions

**Positive:** Feeling of belonging, always arising when something gains meaning. The Guru is actually dependent on this feeling, which is why they have a strong sense of calling, purpose.

**Negative:** Melancholy, void

## Abstract Perception

For eternity, abstract perception is characteristic—the view of oneself, one's life from the outside. The Guru's ability to "look from the outside" allows them to easily see patterns, recurring events.

## Famous Examples

- Ashley Wilkes from "Gone with the Wind" (ВПНБ)
- Herbert Wells (ВБПН)
