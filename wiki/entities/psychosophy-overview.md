# Psychosophy Overview

**Typology:** Psychosophy  
**Level:** Operational  
**Frame Type:** Action frame  
**Source:** Alexander Yuryevich Afanasyev  
**Book:** "Syntax of Love" (Синтаксис Любви)

## What is Psychosophy?

Psychosophy is a typology theory that describes how your psyche relates to four aspects of life: **Volition (Will), Emotions, Logic, and Physics**. It explains how you interact with the world and how this affects your relationships with others.

**24 types** exist in this theory, plus an unnamed 25th type.

The original name was **"Psyche-Yoga" (PY)**, later renamed to "Psychosophy".

## The Four Aspects

| Aspect | Russian | "Element" | Description |
|--------|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **Will** | Воля | Spirit | Strength of character, self-worth, social hierarchy |
| **Emotion** | Эмоция | Soul | Feelings, moods, emotional reactions |
| **Logic** | Логика | Mind | Reasoning, ideas, truth |
| **Physics** | Физика | Body | Material world, comfort, physical pleasures |

## The Four Positions

Each aspect appears in one of four positions, determining its role:

| Position | Name | Type | Properties |
|----------|------|------|------------|
| **1st** | Confident | Hammer/Result | Overabundant, monologue, rigid |
| **2nd** | Flexible | River/Process | Normative, dialogue, compassionate |
| **3rd** | Insecure | Ulcer/Process | Deficient, envious, vulnerable |
| **4th** | Indifferent | Trifle/Result | Scant, dependent, unreliable |

## Function Properties

### Result-Oriented Functions (1st, 4th)
Focus on achieving stable states, less attention to processes.

### Process-Oriented Functions (2nd, 3rd)
Focus on ongoing activities, dialogue, and interaction.

### Introverted Functions (1st, 3rd)
Directed toward one's own state and its maintenance.

### Extraverted Functions (2nd, 4th)
Directed outward, interested in others' states.

### Dominant Functions (1st, 3rd)
Act autonomously, initiate independently.

### Adapting Functions (2nd, 4th)
Follow others' lead, adapt to circumstances.

## The 25th Type

Beyond the 24 standard types, Afanasyev describes a theoretical **"25th type"** (2-2-2-2) — a person who has achieved harmony by developing all functions to the level of the second position.
