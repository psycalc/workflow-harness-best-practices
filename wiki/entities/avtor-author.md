# Автор (1 Прошлое)

**Position:** 1st (Target/Целевой)  
**Aspect:** Past (Прошлое)  
**Latent Process:** Deductive — forms identity first, then reads past through it

## Summary

For the Author, past is the domain of self-realization. Their internal question "Who am I?" practically always transforms into "Who do I want to be?"

The person with this active position toward their past acts like an author writing the book of their life — they **form** themselves according to their plan.

## Key Characteristics

- Sees the past as their "wealth" — "My years are my treasure"
- May embark on an adventure just because it would make an elegant plot twist
- Cannot "leave alone" unfinished situations from the past
- Confident that "everything can be fixed" — new page, new chance

## Latent Process

**Deductive movement:** Self-concept → past facts confirm it

The Author first holds an internal thesis about themselves, then reads the past through it.

## Behavioral Markers

- Forms themselves according to their vision
- Seeks experiences, even dangerous ones, if they're interesting
- Has a stable narrative about their identity
- Past events selected to confirm established identity

## In Relationships

- Has stable narrative about own history
- May interpret partner's past through fixed lens
- Values partners who affirm their established identity
- May struggle to see partner's growth/change over time
