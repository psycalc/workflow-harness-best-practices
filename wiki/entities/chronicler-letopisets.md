# Chronicler (Летописец)

The Chronicler, unlike the Author, is in an observant position. They don't invent or self-realize—they **record**. If for the Author, past is the domain of goals, then for the Chronicler, past is a **tool**. The Chronicler's self-realization lies somewhere outside the domain of the past, outside the answer to the question "who am I?"

## Characteristics

- Does not create their own story, but **uses** their history
- Has a good memory
- Can see in the past sources of current events or analogies with what's happening now
- Perceives the past as something natural, not needing to be changed
- Also sees it as an endless storehouse of knowledge about the world
- Loves to talk, reminisce about the past in company with others
- Not obsessed with their own past; gladly recalls and listens about other people

## The Past as Tool

The Chronicler knows their qualities, knows their aptitudes. This allows them to determine what they are capable of and what they are not. They easily revive old connections and use them, never forget successful or unsuccessful experience.

The Chronicler's past is not demanding of them—they don't need to answer the question "who am I?", don't need to think about who they want to become, don't need to finish what they started. Past is for them just a tool for achieving goals, but a reliable, beloved tool that the Chronicler uses often, with pleasure, on occasion and without.

## Famous Example

From "Winnie-the-Pooh and All, All" by A. Latyshev, Tiggers (Chronicler):

Tigger is confident, but unlike the Author, doesn't try in every way to confirm his reputation of an omnivorous creature, choking on what he doesn't like, and doesn't at all suffer from his limitations like the Critic would. He tries, researches himself from the perspective of the past. Learning something new about himself, he honestly acknowledges facts and moves on without any reflection.

At the end, having tried everything his friends eat and all the food from Kanga's supplies, Tigger discovers that he likes fish oil. Roughly by this algorithm, the creative aspect achieves success.
