# Exile (Изгнанник)

The expression "not finding one's place" was possibly invented by the "Exile."

## Characteristics

- Feels that what is happening to them and where/with whom they are now is not the real thing
- Any imperfection of what's happening is perceived tragically
- Feels separated from what's happening
- Wants to run away, feels like a stranger
- Wherever they run, they won't feel "at home" until they consciously make an effort to "connect" to the present
- The main pain of the third present is the feeling of loneliness, separation from the world

## The Exile's Search

Often feels they have no place at this "party of life," that their home is not here. But where is it? They seek the answers to "Where is my world? What do I belong to?" their entire lives.

## Behavior Patterns

- Often separates from what's happening before meeting it face-to-face
- Requires conscious effort to join the present and feel:
  1. What is happening is what should be happening
  2. I am where I need to be and doing what I need to do

- Often fluctuates from detachment to excessive inclusion
- Such "inclusion" resembles imitation of vigorous activity

## Strengths of the Exile

1. **Unceasing search, aspiration** - Such a person is not threatened by sinking into routine, losing main questions in everyday life, or settling for less
2. **Always an intruder** - Their gaze is not dulled, they always somewhat surprise at local customs
3. **Brings fresh perspective** - Worth listening to

## Famous Examples

- Ashley Wilkes from "Gone with the Wind" (both Ashley and Rhett Butler have 3rd Present)
- Nikolai Berdyaev
- Jews (ПВНБ) - historically experienced and continue to experience "exile"
